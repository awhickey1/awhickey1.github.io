Rotary Combustion Engine
National Silver Medalist

My first self-driven project focused on improving the efficiency of an internal combustion engine when I registered for the yearlong SkillsUSA Engineering Technology/Design competition. I started by researching the intriguing concepts of combustion, friction, and heat transfer to invent a rotary combustion engine that theoretically reduced energy losses by 20%

During the competition, I taught myself how to use SolidWorks and a three-dimensional printer to determine my design's feasibility. I presented my research and design to several engineering firms to evaluate my findings and subsequently prepare for the multiple panels within the competition. My work paid off when out of thousands of teams, I was selected Gold Medalist in Arizona and National Silver Medalist in Engineering Technology.

  • National Silver Medalist in the SkillsUSA Engineering Technology/Design competition against hundreds of teams.
  • Invented rotary combustion engine with less moving parts to potential reduce energy losses by 20%.
  • Modeled and fabricated a prototype by self learning how to use SolidWorks and a three dimensional printer.
